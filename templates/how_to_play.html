📖 How to Play

In this game, you and your partner will read one paragraph at a time and label each sentence as either related to geopolitical risk (GPR) or not. You’ll earn points for a paragraph only if all your sentence-level labels exactly match your partner’s. But don’t worry — you and your partner have up to three attempts to get it right for each paragraph!

💡 Important: Each sentence you select will be labeled as 1 (GPR-related). Sentences not selected will be labeled as 0 (not GPR-related).

🌍 What is a Geopolitical Risk (GPR)?

Geopolitical risk refers to the threat, realization, or escalation of adverse events related to wars or terrorism that affect international relations or global business.

Highlight sentences that clearly describe or imply:

  • War-related events: e.g., invasion, armed conflict, military strikes. (Note: Trade wars should not be considered war-related events.)
  • Terrorism: e.g., political violence, bombings, attacks causing instability
✅ A sentence can count as GPR even if it doesn’t name a specific country — as long as it reflects political risks affecting international peace, security, or business.

📖 Instructions

  1. Each group (consisting of two members) will annotate 15,000 paragraphs, one paragraph at a time.
  2. Each group will also review disagreement cases that another team failed to match.
  3. Each paragraph contains multiple sentences.
  4. You must label each sentence individually, not the paragraph as a whole.

💰 Scoring & Compensation

  1. You will earn points only if you and your partner assign the same label to every sentence in a paragraph.
  2. In other words, a perfect match across all sentences in a paragraph counts as one successful match.
  3. Points for each successful match are divided between partners based on relative completion time — the faster you finish, the more points you earn.
  4. Bonus points are awarded for every 100 and 1,000 tasks you complete.
  5. Any paragraph in which you and your partner fail to agree on every sentence label will be forwarded to another team for review.
  6. ⚠️ Mismatches not only cost your team points but also create scoring opportunities for other teams.

Scoring Example: If Bob finishes labeling in 10 seconds and Alice in 20 seconds, Bob receives 2/3 of the point while Alice receives 1/3, based on the inverse of their annotation times.

Compensation Example:

  • 💼 £400 base pay per annotator who completes all tasks
  • 🎁 £800 bonus shared among the 4 players based on points
  • Example: If player scores are split 30%, 25%, 25%, and 20%, then their bonuses would be £240, £200, £200, and £160 respectively

🎯 Tips for Success

  • ✅ To earn points, you and your partner must assign identical labels to every sentence in the paragraph.
  • 🔁 You have up to 3 attempts per paragraph to reach a match.
  • ⚡ The faster you agree, the higher your score!

🔔 Final Reminder

You earn points only when your sentence-level labels match exactly.
