üåç What is a Geopolitical Risk (GPR)?

Geopolitical risk refers to the threat, realization, or escalation of adverse events related to wars, terrorism, or tensions that affect international relations.

Highlight sentences that clearly describe or imply:

  • War-related events: e.g., invasion, armed conflict, military strikes
  • Terrorism: e.g., political violence, bombings, attacks causing instability
  • General Geopolitical Risk: indirect or broad references to political instability that affect cross-border activities, operations, or international relations
